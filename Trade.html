TRADE AGREEMENT

Cameroun is an open economy. It's important for Camerounian businesses to go beyond our borders
explore more opprotunity and expand markets. The trade agreements which the government has signed can help companies to export

A trade agreement is an International agreement between two or more countries to reduce or remove trade barriers
and bring close economic integration

Cameroun has investment and commercial agreements with the European Union, Canada, CHina, Japan, Russia, South Korea, turkey, USA and the United Kingdom. Cameroun ratified the Africa continental Free Trade Agreement in 2020. A bilateral investment treaty(BIT) between Cameroun and and the United States of America entered into force in 1989. The BIT renews automatically underthe terms of the treaty every 10 years. Companies must obtain a foreign government Approval(FGA) letter from the Camerounian government if they wish to invest in Cameroun. In 2009, the government signed the interim Economic Partnership Agreement(EPA) with the EU in order to secure duty-free, quota-free access to the European market in exchange for the gradual removal of duties and quotas. The EU EPA went into force in 2014. Cameoun signed an EPA with the United Kingdom in 2021 to mirror provisions of the EU EPA following the United Kingdom's exit from the European Union. Cameroun lost its Africa Growth and opportunity Act(AGOA) eligiblity in 2020 due to gross violations of Internationally recognized human rights.
